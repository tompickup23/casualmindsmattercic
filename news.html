Funding 31 March 2026

CMM submits bid to the Ending Homelessness Communities Fund

Casual Minds Matter has submitted a bid to the government's Ending Homelessness Communities Fund, seeking support to establish a dedicated Housing Stability and Recovery Hub in Burnley over the next three years.

The proposal builds on the work CMM has been doing since 2022 from its base at Howe Walk in the Charter Walk Shopping Centre. Over the past twelve months, CMM has supported thirty people into accommodation in Burnley, working alongside its housing partner Nurture Housing to place individuals who were rough sleeping, sofa surfing, or at risk of losing their home.

The EHCF bid sets out plans to bring that work onto a more structured footing. The proposed hub would include a housing stability navigator to provide dedicated casework for residents facing homelessness, a tenancy sustainment and peer recovery worker, and a counsellor to offer trauma-informed therapy alongside the housing support. The aim is to reach people before their situation reaches crisis point, offering practical help with housing alongside support for the mental health difficulties that often sit behind it.

Burnley ranks as the fourth most deprived local authority in England on the 2025 Index of Multiple Deprivation, with forty per cent of its neighbourhoods falling within the most deprived ten per cent nationally. Homelessness presentations have been rising year on year, and there is a severe shortage of one-bed social housing stock in the borough. At the same time, waiting lists for NHS talking therapies remain long, leaving a gap that community organisations like CMM are trying to fill.

Dave Burnett, CMM's director, said the bid represents the natural next step for the organisation. "We have spent four years proving that this model works. People walk into the shop because there is no stigma attached to it. They come in for a browse and end up telling us they have nowhere to sleep tonight, or that they have not spoken to anyone in weeks. The EHCF funding would allow us to build proper wraparound support for those people rather than doing what we can with limited resources."

The application was submitted on 31 March 2026. If successful, the project would begin in the autumn and run for three years, with the goal of supporting over four hundred people and helping at least one hundred and twenty into stable accommodation.

CMM will continue to deliver its existing services from Howe Walk while the bid is assessed, including weekly group sessions, one-to-one counselling, and drop-in housing support.

Update 26 March 2026

New signage goes up at Howe Walk

Casual Minds Matter has unveiled new signage at its Howe Walk base in Burnley town centre, bringing the shop front in line with the organisation's refreshed brand identity for 2026.

The updated signage, designed and printed by Lewis Pearce at CMYK Design and Print, features the new "casuALL Minds Matter" wordmark alongside the "ALL Minds Matter" tagline. The bold black lettering on a sage green background replaces the previous green and black design that had been in place since the shop opened.

The rebrand reflects CMM's growth from a small community project into a more established support service. The new identity retains the recognisable green colour palette but introduces cleaner typography and a more professional finish, intended to give the organisation a stronger presence both on the high street and in its funding applications.

"We wanted something that looked confident without losing the approachable feel," said Dave Burnett, CMM's director. "People still need to feel comfortable walking through the door. The shop has always been about removing barriers, and the new look keeps that while showing that we are serious about what we do."

The Howe Walk premises operates as a casual clothing shop alongside its role as a mental health and housing support base. The dual function is central to CMM's model: visitors can browse clothing without any expectation that they are there for support, lowering the threshold for people who might not otherwise seek help.

Services 15 March 2026

New support groups programme launched

Casual Minds Matter has introduced a new programme of support groups, offering both in-store sessions at Howe Walk and online wellbeing groups for the first time.

The in-store groups run weekly on Thursdays, covering a range of topics including grief, anger management, suicide prevention, PTSD, and general wellbeing. A small donation of two pounds is requested to help cover running costs, though no one is turned away for inability to pay.

The online sessions, delivered via Zoom, open up CMM's support to people who cannot attend in person, whether because of mobility issues, anxiety about leaving the house, or caring responsibilities that make travel difficult. It is the first time CMM has offered remote group support, and the organisation says early interest has been encouraging.

Alongside the regular groups, CMM has also launched a six-week Behavioural Awareness and Recovery Programme aimed at individuals affected by substance misuse. The structured programme combines reflective discussion with practical skills development, helping participants to examine patterns of behaviour, strengthen emotional regulation, and build a personalised relapse prevention plan.

Sue Blackshaw, CMM's director of counselling, said the expanded programme reflects what people have been asking for. "We kept hearing from people that they wanted more than a one-off conversation. They wanted something ongoing, something they could commit to week by week. These groups give people that structure while keeping everything in a setting that feels safe and informal."

Bookings for both the in-store and online groups can be made through the CMM website or by calling 01282 222290.

The Howe Walk base doubles as a drop-in centre and clothing shop, keeping the threshold low for anyone who needs support.

Community 10 March 2026

Local family raises over five hundred pounds in memory of loved one

A Burnley family has raised over five hundred pounds for Casual Minds Matter in memory of Stevie Brown, with the donation set to fund approximately ninety group therapy sessions for local people.

The family, who have committed to fundraising throughout 2026, made their first donation of five hundred and twenty pounds after a series of personal fundraising efforts. The money will go directly towards supporting people attending CMM's group sessions, which cover grief, suicide prevention, PTSD, general wellbeing, and recovery from substance misuse.

A Family Fun Day is planned for 31 May at Padiham Football Club, where the family hopes to continue raising money and awareness. CMM will attend the event to speak about its work and connect with more people in the community. The organisers are welcoming donations of raffle prizes and tombola items in the lead-up to the day.

Dave Burnett said the support means a great deal to the organisation. "When a family channels something so painful into helping other people, that tells you everything about why this community matters. Five hundred and twenty pounds does not sound like a headline figure, but in our setting it goes a long way. That is ninety sessions where someone can sit in a room and talk about what they are going through. You cannot put a price on that."

Anyone wishing to organise their own fundraising event for CMM, or to make a donation, can get in touch via the contact details on this website.

The Howe Walk shop front. Community fundraising helps keep services running at a cost of just £5.78 per session.

Community 20 February 2026

World traveller raises funds for CMM while documenting mental health journey

Matthew Largey has sold his belongings and set off on a round-the-world trip, raising money for Casual Minds Matter along the way while documenting his own mental health journey.

Matthew, who is sharing his travels on social media under the name TravelADHD, said the trip is partly about looking after his own wellbeing and partly about giving something back to an organisation that supports people going through their own challenges.

"This journey is something I need to do for myself, but I also want it to mean something more," he said. "If raising awareness and funds can help even one person get the support they need, then it will mean a lot."

His fundraising page is open for donations, with all proceeds going to CMM's work in Burnley. The journey can be followed on Instagram at @travel.adhd.
