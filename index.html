About Us

A trusted front door for people in need

Established in January 2022, Casual Minds Matter C.I.C. is a Burnley-based community interest company. We provide a low-threshold, accessible first point of contact for people facing mental health difficulties, housing instability, debt, isolation, and the wider pressures of the cost-of-living crisis.

Our approach is simple: meet people where they are, offer practical help without bureaucratic barriers, and support them before their situation becomes a crisis. We combine professional counselling with hands-on housing casework, group support, and community-based recovery — all under one roof at Howe Walk.

What makes us different is how people find us. Our Howe Walk base operates as a casual clothing shop — people can walk in without feeling labelled. There is no clinical waiting room, no stigma attached to visiting. This low-threshold model means we reach people who would never walk into a traditional mental health service.

Mental Health & Wellbeing

Counselling, trauma-informed therapy, Brainspotting, The Rewind Technique, and peer-led group support in a calm, non-clinical environment.

Housing & Homelessness

Housing stability casework, tenancy sustainment, move-on support, and practical help with benefits, debt, identification and referrals.

Community Recovery

Alcohol and substance misuse workshops, peer-led recovery groups, social prescribing partnerships, and community-based engagement.

What We Do

Support that meets people where they are

Counselling & Trauma Therapy

Professional, qualified counselling including specialist Brainspotting and The Rewind Technique for trauma. Available in a calm, private setting at Howe Walk — not a clinical waiting room.

  • One-to-one counselling sessions
  • Trauma-informed therapeutic approaches
  • Clinical supervision available

Housing Stability & Casework

We help people access housing through our partnership with Nurture Housing, support tenants with wellbeing and practical issues, and provide assistance with benefits, debt and identification.

  • Housing search and placement
  • Tenancy sustainment support
  • Benefits, debt and ID assistance

Group Support & Recovery

Regular in-store and online group sessions addressing mental health, substance misuse, social isolation, and recovery. A space where people can connect, share, and build confidence.

  • Weekly Thursday in-store support groups
  • Online wellbeing groups via Zoom
  • 6-week Behavioural Awareness & Recovery Programme
  • Grief, PTSD, suicide prevention & general wellbeing

Referrals & Practical Help

We help people access the right services — whether that means navigating benefits, connecting with substance misuse support, or finding domestic abuse services. No wrong door.

  • Multi-agency referral pathways
  • Benefits and debt navigation
  • Domestic abuse & discrimination support

Our Space

Howe Walk, Burnley

A visible, accessible support base in the heart of Burnley town centre. Open door, no appointment needed.

Drop-in Support

Walk-in access for anyone needing support. No referral required. Our clothing shop front means people can visit without feeling labelled.

Private Counselling Rooms

Calm, private spaces for one-to-one counselling and therapeutic work. Designed for comfort, not clinical formality.

Group & Community Space

Flexible space for peer support groups, workshops, and community activities. The heart of CMM's recovery programme.
