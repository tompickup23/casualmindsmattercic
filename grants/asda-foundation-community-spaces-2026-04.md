### **1. Organisation Description** *(198 words)*

Casual Minds Matter C.I.C. (CMM) is a Burnley-based Community Interest Company delivering trauma-informed mental health and homelessness support. Registered in England & Wales (13829906), we operate from 4 Howe Walk—a frontline hub at St James’s House—providing accessible, stigma-free services to some of Burnley’s most marginalised groups.

Our work centres on **homelessness prevention, mental health recovery, and community cohesion**. We offer one-to-one counselling (incorporating EMDR, Brainspotting, and Rewind Technique), group trauma workshops, housing casework, and tenancy sustainment support. Partnerships with Nurture Housing enable us to secure accommodation for clients facing street homelessness, sofa surfing, or domestic instability. In 2025, we permanently housed **30 people in Burnley**, with 61% of our clients presenting multiple support needs and 40% facing three or more intersecting challenges.

CMM’s model prioritises **low-cost, high-impact interventions**: our group sessions cost just £5.78 per attendee, and our Facebook community reaches over 3,100 local residents. As a grassroots organisation, we are uniquely positioned to address Burnley’s dual crises of homelessness and poor mental health—where 63% of homelessness presentations are single people, and prevention rates are among the highest in Lancashire.

This project will refurbish **4 Howe Walk** to create a **safe, accessible, and trauma-informed community space** that strengthens mental health support and homelessness prevention in Burnley. The refurbishment will address critical gaps in privacy, safety, and capacity, enabling CMM to deliver services more effectively to individuals and groups who currently face barriers to accessing support.

**Key improvements** include:
- **Reconfiguring the reception and counselling rooms** to ensure privacy for sensitive discussions (e.g., domestic abuse, veteran trauma).
- **Installing soundproofing and soft furnishings** to reduce sensory overload for neurodivergent clients and those with PTSD.
- **Upgrading the communal area** to support group work (e.g., trauma workshops, alcohol/substance misuse sessions) and reduce isolation.
- **Enhancing accessibility** with step-free access, clear signage, and a welcoming entrance to reduce stigma for first-time visitors.

This aligns with the **Asda Foundation’s Local Community Spaces Fund** priorities by:
- **Repairing and renovating** a vital local hub to meet community needs.
- **Creating a safe space** that reduces loneliness and isolation (a top concern in Lancashire).
- **Enabling group delivery** to expand reach and impact.

Without this investment, CMM’s ability to deliver **trauma-informed, person-centred support** will be constrained by inadequate facilities, limiting our capacity to serve Burnley’s most vulnerable residents.

- **63% of homelessness presentations are single people**—a group often overlooked by traditional services (Burnley Borough Council, 2025).
- **61% of homeless individuals have at least one support need**, and **40% face three or more intersecting challenges** (e.g., mental ill-health, substance misuse, domestic abuse) (MHCLG, 2024).
- **Loneliness and isolation** are top concerns for Lancashire residents, with **42% reporting feelings of disconnection** (Asda Foundation Community Insight Survey, 2025).

CMM’s clients reflect these trends:
- **Street homeless individuals** and **sofa surfers** (often invisible to services).
- **Veterans** and **domestic abuse survivors** requiring trauma-informed care.
- **People facing discrimination** (e.g., due to race, disability, or LGBTQ+ identity) who need a **non-judgemental, accessible space**.

Current barriers at 4 Howe Walk include:
- **Lack of privacy** in counselling rooms, deterring clients from sensitive discussions.
- **Inadequate group space**, limiting our ability to run trauma workshops or peer support sessions.
- **Poor accessibility**, excluding neurodivergent individuals or those with mobility challenges.

This refurbishment will directly address these gaps, ensuring CMM can **deliver high-quality support** to those who need it most.
